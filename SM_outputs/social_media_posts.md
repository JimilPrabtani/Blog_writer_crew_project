I have designed these posts to target three distinct personas: the **struggling learner**, the **career-climber**, and the **hiring manager**.

### Option 2: LinkedIn (The "Professional Growth" / Narrative)
**Target Audience:** Junior-to-Mid Level Engineers & Career Switchers.
**Goal:** Position the blog as a strategic career guide that emphasizes "T-Shaped" skills.

**Post Content:**

Stop being a "Cloud Tourist." ☁️✈️

Many engineers make the mistake of trying to learn AWS, Azure, and GCP simultaneously to look "more marketable." In reality, this leads to superficial knowledge that doesn't survive a technical interview.

The market now values **T-Shaped Skills**: deep expertise in one provider, with a broad understanding of others. Once you master the first principles of compute, storage, and identity in one ecosystem, switching to another is trivial.

But there's a bigger trap: **Click-Ops.**

If you are still manually clicking buttons in the console to deploy infrastructure, you aren't doing cloud engineering—you're doing cloud administration. In a production environment, manual configuration is a liability.

The shift from "Click-Ops" to "Code-Ops" (IaC) is what separates the amateurs from the pros.
